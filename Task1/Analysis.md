# Task 1 - Campaign Viability Analysis
## Analysis & Observations

## 1. Business Health Analysis

GlowNest appears to be supported by advertising rather than fully dependent on it. The brand generates 2000 organic sales units compared to 1500 ad-attributed units, which suggests that the business already has some level of customer trust, organic visibility, and marketplace presence on Amazon.

The difference between organic and ad-driven margins mainly comes from customer acquisition costs through advertising. However, even after considering advertising spend, Amazon fees, product costs, and fixed operational costs, the business still remains profitable. This indicates that the current business model is financially sustainable.

Another important observation is that the organic sales volume is relatively strong for a premium skincare product. This may indicate repeat purchases, positive customer experience, stronger reviews, and increasing brand familiarity among customers.


---

## 2. Advertising Efficiency Analysis

GlowNest currently maintains a healthy ACOS for a premium skincare category. The company spends approximately ₹200 in advertising cost per ad-attributed customer while generating ₹800 in revenue per unit.

The current TACOS is significantly lower than ACOS, which is a positive sign. This suggests that advertising is not only generating direct sales but may also be contributing to broader organic growth over time.

If the brand continues improving organic visibility while maintaining stable advertising costs, TACOS may improve further in the long term. This would indicate that the business is becoming less dependent on paid advertising and more supported by brand recognition and repeat purchases.

The current advertising strategy appears efficient because the brand is still generating positive contribution margins after all major costs.


---

## 3. Customer & Brand Insights

GlowNest operates in the premium skincare segment, where customer trust, reviews, product quality, and repeat purchase behavior are highly important.

In categories like skincare, the first purchase often acts as customer acquisition, while future purchases can become more profitable through repeat orders and organic traffic. Because of this, slightly higher advertising costs can still be acceptable if customer retention remains strong.

The higher level of organic sales may also indicate:
- growing customer trust
- better marketplace ranking
- repeat customers
- stronger product-market fit

This creates a healthier long-term business position compared to brands that rely entirely on paid advertising.


---

## 4. Scaling & Strategic Considerations

The scaling scenario suggests diminishing returns at higher advertising spends. While increasing ad spend can increase sales volume, the growth is not proportional to the increase in advertising investment.

This indicates that GlowNest should scale advertising steadily rather than aggressively. Rapid increases in ad spend may reduce efficiency if customer acquisition costs continue rising.

If ACOS worsens significantly over time, the company should focus on:
- improving conversion rates
- optimizing targeting
- reducing wasted spend
- strengthening product listing quality
- improving retention and repeat purchase behavior

The business currently appears healthy, but maintaining profitability while scaling will require balancing growth with advertising efficiency.


---

## 5. Key Takeaways

- GlowNest currently has a profitable Amazon business model.
- Organic sales are strong and reduce dependency on paid advertising.
- Advertising efficiency is healthy for a premium skincare brand.
- TACOS suggests that ads may be supporting long-term brand growth.
- Scaling should be gradual because higher spend may lead to diminishing returns.
- Long-term success will likely depend on customer retention, repeat purchases, and maintaining strong organic visibility.
